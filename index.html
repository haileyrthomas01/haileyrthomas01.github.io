About Me

Hi! I'm Hailey, a third-year Computer Science student @ the University of Houston - Clear Lake

I plan on graduating in May 2024 with a Bachelor of Computer Science. For the past seven years, I've been academically determined and willing to make sure I achieve greatness and go the extra mile. Some of my strengths include being a hard worker, dedicated, self-motivated, organized, and working well under pressure. I'm currently interested in Data Analytics and earning certifications while getting my degree.

While I am currently attending UHCL, I am a member of the Black Student Association and the National Society for Black Engineers. I am currently learning more about Computer Science and coding. I am also attending various women in STEM conferences, finding internships to find my passion in Computer Science, and coding projects to gain experience and learn more about programming.

During middle school, I attended the Young Women's College Preparatory Academy. They highly encouraged young women to pursue careers in STEM, so from a young age I was interested in STEM careers. I took an Information Technology class in eighth grade and my sophomore year, a PAP Computer Science course my junior year, and an AP Computer Science A to help further my interests in Computer Science and get more experience with coding. With my curiosity for Computer Science, I took edX courses, worked on coding on Minecraft with my school, and coded on code.org many times during school. I graduated in May 2021 from Shadow Creek High School with a GPA of 3.67, where I was also a Dual Credit student attending Alvin Community College.

2019 Purdue IUPUI P.O.W.E.R. (Preparing Outstanding Women For Engineering Roles) Senior Academy

POWER Senior Academy gives rising high school senior girls the opportunity to see what it is like being an engineering major at IUPUI! Campers will learn about applying for college, finding scholarships, what to expect in collegiate level classes, and more. POWER Senior Academy also places an emphasis on research, with each camper participating in a week-long research project with some of IUPUI’s award-winning faculty and staff.
